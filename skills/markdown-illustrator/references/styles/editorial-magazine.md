# editorial-magazine

Refined, sophisticated electronic-magazine layout with serif typography and layered paper backgrounds

## Design Aesthetic

High-quality magazine feature layout inspired by Kinfolk, Monocle, and Cereal publications. It prioritizes quiet, slow, and humanistic visual storytelling. It relies heavily on generous margins, serif typography, fine lines, and procedural backgrounds to create a sense of tactile print.

## Background

- Color: Warm Paper (#F3F0E8), Warm Sage (#F5F1E8), Kraft Brown (#EEDFC7), Warm Straw (#F0E6D2), Midnight Black (#0E0D0C)
- Texture: Layered paper grain with procedural ink wash or contour line grids

## Color Palette

Requires one of the magazine theme presets: Ink Classic, Indigo Porcelain, Forest Ink, Kraft Paper, Dune, or Midnight Ink.

## Visual Elements

- High-contrast Noto Serif SC/Playfair Display headings with wide tracking (+.04em, weight 500)
- Serif body text (Noto Serif SC) or italic-tolerant bilingual text
- Large, clean rectangular photo wells (aspect ratios like 3:4, 4:3, 3:2)
- High-contrast pull quotes with em-italic English sub-text
- Marginalia columns (left/right sidebar annotations)
- Magazine-style ledger rows (Field Ledger) with thin rule dividers
- Bottom/top issue rows or issue strips (e.g. Vol. 01, Vol. 02)
- Delicate hairline rules (1px) for grouping content

## Style Rules

### Do

- Apply the "larger = lighter" typography stance: keep display titles at weight 500, never bold (700+).
- Use a layered background with paper grain and ink/contour textures.
- Manually hard-break CJK titles at semantic seams to prevent awkward wrapping.
- Align content to a strict asymmetric magazine column grid.
- Keep total colored pixels under 10% (accent color is used only for section tags, page numbers, or key words).

### Don't

- Use heavy, bold sans-serif titles (no "infographic banner" look).
- Mix styles (do not load Swiss fonts or Swiss card blocks).
- Use flat solid colors for backgrounds without grain or atmosphere.
- Add rounded corners, box shadows, or gradient fills.
- Use emojis or decorative SVG circles/drops.

## Best For

Outdoor and travel narratives, personal journals, book/film reviews, design retrospectives, technology essays, and reflective thought-leadership posts
